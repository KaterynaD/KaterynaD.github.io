What Women Talk About

Russian women or even more specific middle-aged, mostly middle-class women from Moscow.

 

 

Topic modeling based on the content of Hotline and Kids Psychology and Development Russian women forum eva.ru

Of course, about Love. The model separated 5 topics one way, or another related to Love, Sex, Male partners, Dating, Marriage and Wedding. All together they are the most popular questions in the forum. Sometimes it`s difficult to assign a specific topic that`s why I joined them all together as the most popular topic.

Love, sex, love affairs, male partners suitable not just for sex but for marriage, life in legal marriage versus concubinage, weddings and divorces, infidelity are international topics and can be discuss by any nationality. However, you may notice I use male partner instead of just “partner``. You hardly can find any topic about the same sex partner relationships in this forum.

 

 

“They`re people like any other people…only the housing problem has corrupted them…``

Mikhail Bulgakov, The Master and Margarita,1937

The second most discussable topic is Housing, even the first one, if not joining together all topics related to Love. That`s maybe something more specific to only Moscow women. After all, buying a house or rather an apartment is still considered as the most reliable investment. If there is a family problem, and the topic starter did not mention this in a clear way in the first post, the first reply is always “Who is the owner of the apartment?``. You are more likely to find a partner, succeed in a family crisis if you own your place to live or, at least, able to rent or get mortgage. The best what you can do for your kids for start an adult life is their own apartments. On the other hand, you need be very careful if a partner of your child does not have an own place or, even worse, moved in Moscow from another region. If you own an extra apartment, you always have additional money from rent and, it`s often the only plan for wealthy retirement. Owning an apartment in Moscow allows to live and do not work in many other cities even in other countries.

 

Job and Career. In short, the most popular discussion in this category is “What can I do now after 5 – 10 – 15 years of maternity leave, no experience and 2 diplomas from top universities?``. Sometimes there are complains about work forces from other regions who are ready to work for 2 cents, doing everything as slaves which are hired more eagerly than inexperienced Moscow natives. A lot of desperate women with kids after or on the edge of divorce are not protected by law. I exaggerated it a little bit but there are clear lessons for young women that everything can happen in a currently happy and secured life.

Older women may lose their jobs few years before the official age of retirement and it`s practically impossible to find a new job in this age if you are an ordinary employee without highly demand skills or relations to big bosses. Sometimes women are just burn-out on the same job for years and looking for advices in what new area they can find themselves in the age 40+. There are also complains about husbands who lost a job and even do not try to find a new one.

 

Interesting, in the mind of Russian women, a proper career is only a manager position. It is not clear from translated to English word map, but in Russian, original version, different forms, and synonyms of word “manager`` are dominant. The reason is that managers always have higher salary and a more secure position.

Inheritance. Since the most valuable is a parent`s (or grandparents) apartment, the usual question is how to divide an inherited apartment between heirs. “Why he/she did not care about old parents but get everything?`` or “How can I sell only my own part of the inherited apartment if my brother/sister does not want to buy or do not have money to buy?``

Domestic violence. Surprisingly a lot of discussion in this forum.

Apathy and mental health problems. Sometimes “Groundhog Day`` is a usual way of events for moms spending all time at home with kids causing boredom, apathy and depression. The forum plays a role of a shrink to listen or advice a real specialist in more serious cases. Close topics are Doctors, Medical Examinations, and Diseases.

Considering the age and income level of the most forum participants, travels topics are more popular than “dacha``. And vegetables gardens are discussed mostly from the point of view unneeded food provided from mothers and, especially, mothers in low.

Other topics: Celebrations and Gifts, Polls about everything and life in other countries specifically, Food, House Cleaning, Pregnancy, Childbirth, Abortion and Adoption, Social Media, Virtual communication, Texting and Phone conversations, Alcoholism, Neighbors, Pets.

Data Source

eva.ru forums exist for more than 10 years. You need to register to publish your posts, but you can do it anonymously. Even your virtual identity is known only to forum administrators and readers cannot connect various anonymous posts to one person. Due to the anonymity hundreds of women freely discuss sensitive topics. However, there are a lot of sarcasm and even aggression due to the anonymity. The site is the only place where many women can spill out disappointment in life, spouses, kids, parents, jobs etc. Most of replies are negative (except Kids Psychology and Development) and “It happened to you because you are dummy and do not deserve better!`` is a usual reaction for most concerns.

Topics in forums are built as dialogs in a tree-form. A topic starter publishes a first post and other women reply, ask additional questions directly to this first post or organize separate branches starting from replies to the first post.

There is practically no limit on the length of posts and because participants are usually educated, middle-aged persons there are almost no gibberish. Profanity and especially aggressive posts are deleted by moderators.

I choose eva.ru forums as a data source because I read forums for years as a relax between coding and to keep up to date my Russian. I know what women talking about in these forums and can estimate how good topic modeling results are even without special metrics. I was also interested in a review of available NLP libraries for Russian language.

Technical aspects and lessons learned.

For topic modeling first post from each topic forum was downloaded and cleaned up from links, images and emojis.

Average length of first posts in Hotline forum is 70 words and in Kids Psychology and Development is 50 words. Short posts, less then 5 words, were not considered because usually it`s a service forum topic, created by a forum`s administrator.

Number of topics in Hotline forum is almost 48,000 and in Kids Psychology and Development is 21,000.

I conducted few experiments (although, not very thorough) to find a better model and a topics set.

3 different topic modeling libraries with different parameters:

1.       BerTopics

a.       Simple approach with just language="Russian"

b.       Few different embedding models (embedding_model="blinoff/roberta-base-russian-v0", etc.)

2.       LDA: variety combinations of alpha, beta and num_topics parameters

3.       Top2Vec

a.       embedding_model="doc2vec"

b.       embedding_model="universal-sentence-encoder-multilingual"

c.       embedding_model="distiluse-base-multilingual-cased"

3 stages of text preprocessing:

1.       Raw text, no preprocessing, only links, images and emojis removed

2.       Tokenization, removing stop words, and lemmatization

3.       Tokenization, lemmatization, and bigrams

The best model and topic set was selected based on coherence score and manual review of the topic sets.

Sets of parameters, dataset file names and column names to use in the experiments were configured in a Google Sheets file. The code loops over parameters, creates a model, topics and calculates coherence score metric. Coherence and topic sets from each model are saved back to the same file. I like this simple and straightforward approach better than using hardcoded ranges of parameters or json configuration files. I can use not only numeric parameters in an organize way, clear configure only specific combination of parameters I want to investigate, and then review and compare an associated to parameters score metric and topic sets in a simple table from all trials together in one place.

The most frustrated experience I received from BerTopic because the results are NOT reproduceable. The models returned about 25 topics from Kids Psychology and Development forum, but most of the forum posts were in the first “garbage`` category and other categories were each time different. There is no way to stabilize the model result e.g., get the same result with the same embedding model and data, and this behavior is considered as an advantage of the BerTopic because you can choose a topic categories you like most.

With Hotline forum posts I had even worse experience. First test run produced results comparable to the results from Kids Psychology and Development, but I was not able achieve the same result anymore never matter how many times I tried the same or different embedding models. Each model returned no more then 5 topics with most of posts in the first “garbage`` category.

The main difference between Kids Psychology and Development and Hotline is variety of the topics. Kids Psychology and Development is more specialized, and Hotline is just about everything. Looks like BerTopic works better with more specific texts.

LDA. This is an old and well documented library. It does not use modern transformers approach and works fast. Unfortunately, it does not work well with wide variety of topic categories. LDA best results at the level of 10 topics and, of course, there is one topic category where very hard to identify what this about based on the topic words. That`s the largest topic category but I would also consider it as a “garbage``.

What I like about LDA is visualization and calculation a list of topic probabilities for each document, so I can interpret the topic category of a post as the category with highest probability for that post.

Top2Vec provides the best results with Doc2Vec embedding model. There are almost 300 original topic categories from Hotline forum posts and 140 from Kids Psychology and Development. There is a functionality to reduce number of topics. Coherence metric (the higher the better) was increasing till some number of reduced topic sets and then started decreasing. I choose a number of topics with the highest coherence metric, played with numbers around this value, manually reviewing the topics words. Finally, I created 35 topics in Hotline and 20 in Kids Psychology and Development. Looking at these categories I can say I would create the same set manually if I had time and desire. However, I am not sure about the number of posts assigned to each category. I can not review all thousands of texts manually. The numbers of posts in each category are suspiciously close to each other.

What`s missing in Top2Vec is a good visualization. The way, how visualization is done in BerTopic can be adopted easily for this library.

Top2Vec documentations claims that text pre-processing is not needed. As an example, stop words because they are present in each text, will be removed automatically. Here is what I found practically:

1.       Yes, stop words were removed. But there is another category. Words like “kid``, “children``, “girl``, “boy``, “daughter`` or “son`` are very popular in Kids Psychology and Development forum posts. They were not removed but extensively used in topics. From my point of view, it`s a noise. Topics became more readable when I removed these words before modeling. I did not observe such issue in Hotline forum posts.  

2.       Lemmatization is a must for Russian text. Without it, topic words full of the same words in different forms. I used pymorphy2 library and sometimes I received, well, strange word forms.

3.       Bigrams. Very nice to have. It`s more readable “gift, New Year, amount, money, ask, Grandfather Frost, Birthday`` then “new, gift, money, year, day, grandfather, amount, Frost, ask, birth`` (Traditionally, Russian kids receive gifts from Grandfather Frost aka Santa Clause on New Year eve, not Christmas and Birthday is 2 words in Russian). Better quality bigrams are created after lemmatization. pymorphy2 deforms bigrams if I do it in other direction.

Bottom line: text pre-processing makes topics more understandable in Russian language.

What`s missed in all topic modeling packages is automatic creation of topics names. Yes, it`s quite clear what this all about looking at the topic words, but for charts and summaries I need short names. I found it`s challenging to do it myself. Abstractive text summarization is a promising approach but challenging, especially, for Russian language.

Coherent metric is good enough to compare models created using the same approach. Looks like unusable to compare models from LDA and Top2Vec.
